Honda B-series: A Tribute

1988-2001

What's so special about the B-series?

The B-series classification of engines designed by the Honda Motor Company are a family of inline-four DOHC (Double Overhead Cam) engines intended for automotive application. They were performance engines marking the first implementation of the VTEC (Variable Valve Timing and Lift Electronic Control) system enabling the engines such as the B-16A to boast an impressive 100 horsepower per litre of displacement through natural aspiration. It is no wonder that they were seen as engineering marvels.

Here's some information about the B-Series engines

  • Cylinder Configuration: Inline 4-Cylinder
  • Valvetrain: 4-Valve DOHC VTEC
  • Bore: 81 mm (B16, B17, B18), 84 mm (B20)
  • Stroke: 77.4 mm (B16), 81.4 mm (B17), 89 mm (B18, B20)
  • Cylinder Block Material: Cast Aluminium Alloy
  • Cylinder Head Material: Cast Aluminium Alloy
